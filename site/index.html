How Washington buys AI

There is no single number for federal AI spending. The number is a choice.

Headline finding

Change the word list and the count changes. The reality does not.

I ran the same pull twice with two different word lists. Two phrases flag 1,580 of the awards. Nine phrases flag 1,947. The extra 367, about 19 percent of the total, exist only because the list is wider. Nothing about the contracts changed. Only the definition did.

The question

Does the definition decide the answer?

How much the reported count depends on how you define an AI contract, and what the standard keyword method cannot see.

The approach

Open data, open rule.

Every input is a public record. The rule that decides what counts as AI lives in one file, in the open. Change the rule and the result changes.

The data

2,107 awards, cross-checked.

2,107 contract awards pulled, 1,947 classified as AI in window. Checked against 3,611 reported AI use cases, 1,023 of them acquired by contract.
