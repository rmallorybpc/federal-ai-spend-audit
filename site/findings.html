Key findings

Four findings

What the open data shows about how federal AI gets counted.

Finding 1

The count depends on the words you pick.

Two phrases returned 1,600 awards. Nine phrases returned 1,979, a 24 percent rise from word choice alone. Every headline figure for federal AI spending rests on a word list like this one. Most do not show you the list.

Finding 2

Two methods find two different governments.

The keyword method shows a defense research world. The inventory shows a civilian one. Put the top names side by side and almost none appear on both.

Finding 3

The top AI vendor is a measurement artifact.

MathWorks is the largest AI contractor by the keyword method. MathWorks makes MATLAB. Those awards are very likely tool and license purchases that match because the words machine learning sit in the product description. A tool license is not an AI program. The keyword method cannot tell the difference.

Finding 4

Eighteen agencies the keyword method cannot see.

These agencies report AI they bought by contract. None show up in the keyword results.
